## What is Integrated BioRisk Assessment?

Integrated BioRisk assessment is a comprehensive risk evaluation methodology that combines biological safety and security risks into a unified framework. The BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk) tool represents the evolution of separate biosafety and biosecurity assessments into an integrated approach that recognizes the interdependence of safety and security measures in laboratory environments.

Rather than treating biosafety and biosecurity as separate domains, the integrated approach evaluates how safety and security measures interact, identifying opportunities for synergy and avoiding conflicts between safety and security objectives.

<Note>
BioRAM-2022 is available in Excel format and represents the newest version of the assessment methodology (as of December 2023 update). The tool joins safety and security assessment into a single, streamlined evaluation process.
</Note>

### Optimized Risk Management

**Dual-Benefit Measures**

Many risk mitigation measures provide both safety and security benefits:

- **Access control systems** prevent unauthorized entry (security) and limit exposure potential (safety)
- **Inventory tracking** ensures material accountability (security) and prevents accidental use of wrong materials (safety)
- **Training programs** build competency (safety) and security awareness (security)
- **Incident reporting** captures both exposures (safety) and security events (security)

**Conflict Resolution**

Integrated assessment identifies and resolves potential conflicts:

- Emergency egress requirements vs. access control
- Laboratory visibility for supervision vs. security through obscurity
- Collaboration and openness vs. need-to-know restrictions
- Convenience and workflow vs. security controls

<Tip>
The integrated approach helps find solutions that satisfy both safety and security requirements, rather than creating compromises that weaken one objective to satisfy the other.
</Tip>

The integrated approach aligns with current biorisk management frameworks:

- ISO 35001:2019 Biorisk management systems
- National biosafety and biosecurity frameworks emphasizing integration

## How Safety and Security Risks Interact

Understanding the interactions between safety and security is fundamental to integrated biorisk management:

### Complementary Relationships

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Access Control" icon="door-closed">
    **Safety Benefit**: Limits number of potentially exposed personnel, restricts access based on training and competency
    
    **Security Benefit**: Prevents unauthorized access, creates accountability for material access
    
    **Integrated Optimization**: Role-based access considers both training/competency (safety) and authorization level (security)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Inventory Management" icon="clipboard-list">
    **Safety Benefit**: Ensures proper storage conditions, prevents use of expired or contaminated materials, supports waste tracking
    
    **Security Benefit**: Material accountability, theft detection, audit capability
    
    **Integrated Optimization**: Real-time inventory systems serve both safety and security with single data infrastructure
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Containment Design" icon="cubes">
    **Safety Benefit**: Prevents release to environment and exposure of personnel
    
    **Security Benefit**: Physical barriers to unauthorized access and removal
    
    **Integrated Optimization**: Facility design incorporates both inward protection (safety) and outward protection (security)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Personnel Programs" icon="users">
    **Safety Benefit**: Training in safe procedures, health surveillance, competency verification
    
    **Security Benefit**: Personnel screening, security awareness, insider threat mitigation
    
    **Integrated Optimization**: Comprehensive personnel program addresses both safety competency and security reliability
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Potential Tensions

Some safety and security requirements may create tensions that require careful balance:

| Consideration | Safety Priority | Security Priority | Integrated Solution |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Emergency egress** | Rapid escape from exposures | Controlled exit to prevent theft | Balanced design with breakaway access during true emergencies |
| **Information sharing** | Collaboration and consultation | Need-to-know restrictions | Tiered information sharing based on risk level |
| **Visibility** | Supervision and oversight | Limited information to potential adversaries | Internal visibility with external obscurity |
| **Automation** | Reduce human error | Maintain human oversight | Automated systems with appropriate monitoring |

<Warning>
Integrated biorisk assessment explicitly evaluates these tensions and recommends solutions that appropriately balance safety and security based on the specific risk profile.
</Warning>

## Comprehensive Risk Evaluation

The BioRAM-2022 integrated assessment evaluates risk across all relevant dimensions:

### Assessment Components

**Hazard and Threat Characterization**
- Biological agent properties (pathogenicity, transmissibility)
- Attractiveness for misuse (weaponization potential, availability)
- Dual-use research concerns
- Consequence severity for both accidental and intentional events

**Exposure and Vulnerability Assessment**
- Procedures and activities creating exposure risk
- Volumes and concentrations handled
- Access vulnerabilities and control effectiveness
- Personnel factors in both safety and security

**Consequence Analysis**
- Health impacts of accidental exposures
- Public health impacts of intentional release
- Environmental consequences
- Economic and societal impacts
- Institutional and regulatory consequences

**Risk Mitigation Evaluation**
- Engineering controls (containment, access barriers)
- Administrative controls (procedures, training, oversight)
- Personal protective measures
- Detection and response capabilities
- Recovery and continuity planning

### Integrated Risk Score

The BioRAM-2022 tool calculates an integrated risk score that considers:

```
Integrated BioRisk = f(Safety Risk, Security Risk, Interaction Effects)
```

Where:
- **Safety Risk** = (Hazard × Exposure Probability) / Safety Mitigation
- **Security Risk** = (Threat × Vulnerability) × Consequence
- **Interaction Effects** = Synergies and conflicts between safety and security measures

## When to Use Integrated vs Separate Assessments

While the integrated approach offers many benefits, certain situations may warrant separate assessments:

### Use Integrated Assessment When:

✅ **Initial facility risk assessment** - Comprehensive baseline evaluation

✅ **Regulatory compliance** - Meeting requirements for both safety and security

✅ **Program development** - Establishing new biorisk management program

✅ **Facility design** - Planning new or renovated laboratories

✅ **Periodic reassessment** - Regular comprehensive program evaluation

✅ **Complex operations** - Multiple agents, diverse procedures, high risk levels

### Consider Separate Assessments When:

⚠️ **Highly specialized evaluation needed** - Deep dive into specific safety or security issue

⚠️ **Different regulatory pathways** - Separate compliance documentation required

⚠️ **Distinct expertise required** - Highly technical safety or security analysis

⚠️ **Security classification concerns** - Security assessment requires classified handling

⚠️ **Targeted follow-up** - Reassessment of specific safety or security improvements

<Tip>
**Hybrid Approach**

Many institutions use integrated assessment as the primary tool with targeted separate assessments for specific issues identified during integrated evaluation.
</Tip>

## Using BioRAM-2022

The integrated BioRisk assessment is conducted using the BioRAM-2022 Excel tool:

### Getting Started

1. **Access the tool**: Download BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk)-updated Dec 2023.xlsx from the repository

2. **Review example**: Examine the MonkeyPox example assessment for guidance on completion

3. **Assemble team**: Include safety officer, security officer, laboratory supervisor, and key technical staff

4. **Gather information**: Collect relevant documentation, facility plans, procedures, and records

### Assessment Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Facility and Agent Information">
    Complete identification and characterization sections:
    - Facility details and regulatory context
    - Biological agent(s) and risk classification
    - Operational scope and activities
    - Regulatory and compliance requirements
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Integrated Risk Evaluation">
    Answer comprehensive questionnaire covering:
    - Procedures and exposure scenarios (safety)
    - Access and vulnerability assessment (security)
    - Containment and control measures (both)
    - Personnel programs (both)
    - Emergency preparedness (both)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Generated Results">
    Examine automated risk scoring and analysis:
    - Overall integrated biorisk level
    - Component safety and security scores
    - Key risk drivers and contributing factors
    - Comparison to recommended controls
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Develop Action Plan">
    Use recommendations to create improvement plan:
    - Prioritized corrective actions
    - Timeline and resource requirements
    - Responsibility assignments
    - Implementation monitoring approach
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices for Integrated Assessment

### Assessment Team Composition

Include representatives from all relevant disciplines:
- Biosafety officer
- Security officer or physical security staff
- Laboratory supervisor or principal investigator
- Facilities and engineering staff
- IT/cybersecurity representative
- Occupational health representative
- Institutional biosafety committee member

### Preparation and Planning

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Document Collection">
    - Standard operating procedures
    - Training records and curricula
    - Facility drawings and specifications
    - Equipment certifications and maintenance records
    - Security system documentation
    - Inventory procedures and records
    - Previous assessment reports
    - Incident and near-miss reports
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Stakeholder Engagement">
    - Inform laboratory personnel of assessment purpose and process
    - Schedule time commitments for team members
    - Coordinate with biosafety and security committees
    - Engage institutional leadership for resource decisions
    - Plan for confidential handling of security information
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Completion Tips

✓ **Be thorough**: Answer all questions completely and honestly

✓ **Use evidence**: Reference specific procedures, equipment, and documentation

✓ **Engage expertise**: Consult subject matter experts for technical questions

✓ **Document assumptions**: Note any assumptions or limitations in responses

✓ **Consider both aspects**: For each question, think about both safety and security implications

✓ **Plan for gaps**: Identify areas where information is unavailable for follow-up

### Follow-up and Implementation

1. **Review results** with full assessment team and institutional committees

2. **Prioritize actions** based on risk reduction potential and feasibility

3. **Develop timeline** with clear milestones and responsibilities

4. **Allocate resources** including budget, personnel, and equipment

5. **Implement improvements** with progress monitoring and documentation

6. **Verify effectiveness** through follow-up assessment of implemented measures

7. **Communicate outcomes** to laboratory personnel and institutional leadership

8. **Schedule reassessment** based on triggers or routine cycle

<Warning>
**Reassessment Schedule**

Conduct reassessment when:
- New agents or procedures introduced
- Facility modifications or relocations
- Security incidents or safety events
- Significant personnel changes
- Changes in regulatory requirements
- At minimum every 2-3 years for ongoing operations
</Warning>
