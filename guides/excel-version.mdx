BioRAM provides Excel-based tools for conducting biosafety and biosecurity laboratory risk assessments. This guide will help you choose the right Excel file and perform comprehensive assessments.

## Overview

The Excel versions of BioRAM offer a spreadsheet-based approach to risk assessment, requiring no software installation beyond Microsoft Excel or compatible spreadsheet applications.

### Available Excel Files

BioRAM includes several Excel files for different assessment needs:

- **BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk).xlsx** - The newest integrated tool (beta)
- **BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk)-updated Dec 2023.xlsx** - Latest update with improvements
- **BioRAM-Safety-2016.xlsx** - Standalone biosafety assessment
- **BioRAM-Security-2020.xlsx** - Standalone biosecurity assessment
- **BioRAM-Security-2017.xlsx** - Previous security version
- **BioRAM-Security-2016.xlsx** - Legacy security version

<Note>
The MonkeyPox example files demonstrate completed assessments for a laboratory conducting diagnostics on monkey pox virus.
</Note>

## Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Excel file">
    Download and open your chosen BioRAM Excel file in Microsoft Excel or a compatible application (LibreOffice Calc, Google Sheets with limitations).
    
    <Note>
    Enable macros if prompted, as some calculations may use Excel formulas and features.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the instruction sheet">
    Most BioRAM Excel files begin with an introduction or instructions worksheet. Read this carefully to understand:
    - The assessment methodology
    - How risk scores are calculated
    - Special considerations for your laboratory type
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter facility information">
    Navigate to the facility information section and complete:
    - Laboratory name and location
    - Assessment date
    - Assessor names and roles
    - Laboratory biosafety level (BSL-1, BSL-2, BSL-3, BSL-4)
    - Type of work conducted
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Navigating the Spreadsheet

BioRAM Excel files are organized into multiple worksheets, each serving a specific purpose:

### Common Worksheet Structure

| Worksheet | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|
| **Introduction/Instructions** | Overview of the assessment methodology and guidance |
| **Facility Information** | Basic laboratory details and contact information |
| **Agent Information** | Details about biological agents being assessed |
| **Risk Factors** | Questions and scoring for various risk categories |
| **Risk Assessment** | Automated risk calculations and matrices |
| **Mitigation** | Recommended controls and risk reduction measures |
| **Summary/Report** | Final risk scores and executive summary |

<Note>
Worksheet names and organization may vary slightly between versions. Use the tab names at the bottom of Excel to navigate between sections.
</Note>

## Filling Out Assessment Sections

### Agent Information

For the biological agent(s) being assessed, provide:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the agent">
    - Scientific name (genus and species)
    - Common name
    - Strain or variant if applicable
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Characterize the hazard">
    - Disease caused
    - Transmission route (aerosol, contact, vector-borne)
    - Risk group classification (1-4)
    - Select agent status (if applicable)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Describe the work">
    - Procedures performed (culture, diagnostics, animal work)
    - Quantities handled
    - Frequency of work
    - Number of personnel with access
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Risk Factor Assessment

The risk factor section contains questions organized by category. For each question:

1. **Read the question carefully** - Understand what aspect of risk is being evaluated
2. **Select the appropriate response** - Usually a dropdown menu or radio button
3. **Provide justification** - Document why you selected that response
4. **Attach evidence** - Reference SOPs, training records, or facility documents

#### Biosafety Risk Categories

- **Inherent Agent Risk**: Pathogenicity, virulence, environmental stability
- **Exposure Potential**: Procedures, quantities, aerosol generation
- **Personnel Competency**: Training, experience, supervision
- **Engineering Controls**: Biological safety cabinets, HVAC, directional airflow
- **Administrative Controls**: SOPs, access restrictions, medical surveillance
- **Personal Protective Equipment**: Gloves, lab coats, respirators

#### Biosecurity Risk Categories

- **Consequence of Loss**: Potential for misuse, public health impact
- **Attractiveness to Theft**: Dual-use potential, ease of weaponization
- **Physical Security**: Locks, alarms, intrusion detection
- **Personnel Security**: Background checks, insider threat programs
- **Inventory Management**: Tracking, accountability, reconciliation
- **Information Security**: Access to protocols, data protection

<Warning>
Be honest and thorough in your assessments. Underestimating risks can lead to inadequate controls and potential incidents.
</Warning>

## Understanding Risk Scores and Results

### Risk Calculation

BioRAM uses a matrix-based approach to calculate risk:

```
Risk = Likelihood × Consequence
```

- **Likelihood**: Probability that an exposure or security breach will occur
- **Consequence**: Severity of impact if the event occurs

### Risk Levels

Results are typically categorized as:

| Risk Level | Score Range | Interpretation | Action Required |
|------------|-------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Very Low** | 1-4 | Minimal risk | Standard controls adequate |
| **Low** | 5-9 | Acceptable with current controls | Monitor periodically |
| **Medium** | 10-16 | Moderate risk | Implement additional controls |
| **High** | 17-25 | Significant risk | Immediate mitigation required |

<Note>
Exact scoring ranges may vary between BioRAM versions. Refer to the scoring guide in your specific Excel file.
</Note>

### Risk Matrix Interpretation

The BioRAM 2022 integrated version provides:

- **Combined Biorisk Score**: Overall facility risk profile
- **Biosafety Score**: Safety-specific risk level
- **Biosecurity Score**: Security-specific risk level
- **Priority Areas**: Sections requiring immediate attention

### Reviewing Results

Navigate to the Summary or Report worksheet to view:

1. **Overall risk rating** - Your laboratory's final risk classification
2. **Category breakdown** - Risk scores for each assessment area
3. **Gap analysis** - Controls that are missing or inadequate
4. **Recommended mitigation** - Specific actions to reduce risk

## Mitigation and Risk Reduction

Based on your assessment results, the Excel tool will recommend:

### Control Hierarchy

<Steps>
  <Step title="Elimination">
    Can you eliminate the hazard entirely?
    - Use a less dangerous agent
    - Discontinue unnecessary procedures
    - Reduce quantities to minimum needed
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Substitution">
    Can you substitute with something safer?
    - Use inactivated agents for training
    - Employ simulants for protocol development
    - Consider in vitro vs. animal models
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Engineering Controls">
    Install or upgrade physical safeguards:
    - Biological safety cabinets
    - Sealed centrifuge rotors
    - HEPA filtration
    - Access control systems
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Administrative Controls">
    Implement policies and procedures:
    - Standard operating procedures
    - Training programs
    - Medical surveillance
    - Inventory management systems
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Personal Protective Equipment">
    Provide appropriate PPE (least effective control):
    - Laboratory coats
    - Gloves
    - Eye protection
    - Respirators when required
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Saving and Exporting Reports

### Saving Your Assessment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Save with a descriptive name">
    Use a naming convention that includes:
    ```
    BioRAM-[FacilityName]-[AgentName]-[Date].xlsx
    ```
    
    Example: `BioRAM-Lab3-EcoliO157-2026-03-04.xlsx`
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Maintain version control">
    Keep previous versions when updating assessments:
    - Annual reassessments
    - After facility changes
    - Following incidents or near-misses
    - When adding new agents or procedures
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Store securely">
    Risk assessments may contain sensitive information:
    - Store in access-controlled locations
    - Encrypt if required by policy
    - Back up regularly
    - Limit distribution to need-to-know personnel
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Generating Reports

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="PDF Export">
    To create a permanent record:
    
    1. Select the worksheets to include (use Ctrl+Click to select multiple tabs)
    2. Go to File > Print or File > Export as PDF
    3. Choose "Entire Workbook" or "Selected Sheets"
    4. Configure page layout (landscape often works better)
    5. Save as PDF with a descriptive name
    
    <Note>
    Review the PDF to ensure all data and charts are clearly visible before finalizing.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Summary Report">
    Many BioRAM Excel files include a pre-formatted summary sheet:
    
    1. Navigate to the Summary or Report worksheet
    2. Verify all data has populated correctly
    3. Add any additional narrative or context
    4. Print or export just this sheet for executive summaries
    5. Include recommended mitigation measures
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Data Export">
    To integrate with other systems:
    
    1. Export specific worksheets as CSV files
    2. Copy data to your organization's risk management database
    3. Import into statistical analysis tools if conducting program-wide assessments
    
    <Warning>
    Remove sensitive information before exporting to non-secure systems.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Best Practices

### Conduct Assessments Collaboratively

- Involve laboratory supervisors, biosafety officers, and frontline workers
- Include facility managers for engineering control questions
- Engage security personnel for biosecurity sections
- Consider external reviewers for objectivity

### Document Thoroughly

- Provide specific justifications for each response
- Reference supporting documents (SOPs, training records, facility drawings)
- Note assumptions and limitations
- Record dissenting opinions if team members disagree

### Review and Update Regularly

- Annual reassessments at minimum
- After any significant changes:
  - New agents or procedures
  - Facility modifications
  - Personnel changes
  - Incidents or near-misses
  - Regulatory updates

### Link to Risk Management

- Use assessment results to prioritize improvements
- Track mitigation implementation
- Measure effectiveness of control measures
- Incorporate into institutional biosafety and biosecurity programs

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Formulas showing errors">
    - Ensure macros are enabled
    - Check that all required fields are completed
    - Verify you're using a compatible Excel version (2010 or later)
    - Try opening in Microsoft Excel rather than alternative applications
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Dropdowns not working">
    - Protected worksheets may prevent editing certain cells
    - Look for an "Unprotect Sheet" option in the Review tab
    - Ensure you're clicking in the correct input cells (often highlighted)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Risk scores seem incorrect">
    - Verify all sections are completed
    - Check for hidden worksheets that contain reference data
    - Review the scoring methodology in the instructions
    - Ensure dropdown selections were made (not manual text entry)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Cannot save or export">
    - Check file permissions on the save location
    - Ensure the file isn't open in another application
    - Try "Save As" with a new filename
    - Close and reopen Excel if it becomes unresponsive
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
