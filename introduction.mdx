## What is BioRAM?

BioRAM (Biological Risk Assessment Methodology) is a comprehensive laboratory risk assessment tool developed by Sandia National Laboratories to help facilities evaluate and manage biological safety and security risks. The tool provides structured, quantitative risk assessment methodologies for laboratory environments handling biological agents and hazardous chemicals.

BioRAM enables laboratory professionals to systematically identify, assess, and mitigate risks through a series of guided questionnaires that evaluate facility characteristics, agent properties, operational procedures, and existing safety measures.

## Who should use BioRAM?

BioRAM is designed for laboratory professionals responsible for safety, security, and risk management, including:

- **Laboratory managers** overseeing day-to-day operations of biological or chemical facilities
- **Biosafety officers** responsible for implementing and monitoring safety protocols
- **Biosecurity professionals** managing security measures for biological materials
- **Facility directors** making strategic decisions about laboratory operations and compliance
- **Environmental health and safety (EHS) personnel** conducting institutional risk assessments
- **Regulatory compliance staff** preparing documentation for inspections and audits
- **Research administrators** evaluating risks for new laboratory projects or research programs

<Note>
BioRAM is particularly valuable for facilities that need to demonstrate systematic risk assessment approaches for regulatory compliance, accreditation, or funding requirements.
</Note>

### Tool formats

BioRAM is available in two formats to accommodate different user needs:

**Excel version**
- Familiar spreadsheet interface requiring no additional software
- Self-contained assessment files that can be saved and shared
- Ideal for straightforward assessments and users comfortable with Excel
- Available versions: BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk), BioRAM-Safety-2016, BioRAM-Security (2016-2020)

**Java application**
- Desktop application with advanced features and enhanced reporting
- Multilingual support with translations in Spanish, French, Russian, Indonesian, and Arabic
- Uses `.raml` (Risk Assessment Markup Language) files to define assessment models
- Version 4.0.5 provides the most current Java implementation

### Output and reporting

Both versions generate comprehensive risk assessment reports including:

- Overall risk scores categorized as low, medium, or high
- Detailed analysis by risk category and contributing factors
- Identification of specific vulnerabilities and exposure pathways
- Recommended mitigation measures prioritized by effectiveness
- Documentation suitable for compliance and accreditation purposes

## Brief history and development

BioRAM was developed by Sandia National Laboratories to address the need for quantitative, systematic risk assessment methodologies in biological laboratory settings. The tool has evolved through multiple versions:

- **2016**: Initial releases of separate biosafety and biosecurity Excel tools
- **2017-2020**: Enhanced biosecurity versions with expanded assessment criteria
- **2022**: Introduction of integrated BioRisk assessment combining safety and security
- **Version 4.0+**: Java application with multilingual support and advanced features

The methodology is documented in the official BioRAM User Guide (SAND report), which provides detailed information about the scientific basis, risk calculation methods, and application examples.

<Warning>
The BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk) integrated assessment tool is still in beta. While fully functional, users should review results carefully and provide feedback for continued improvement.
</Warning>

## Example applications

BioRAM has been successfully applied to various laboratory scenarios, including:

- Diagnostic laboratories conducting work with infectious agents (see the Monkey Pox example)
- Research facilities developing new biological products or therapeutics
- Clinical laboratories handling patient specimens
- Biocontainment facilities requiring BSL-3 or BSL-4 operations
- Chemical laboratories storing or using hazardous materials
