## Overview

This case study demonstrates how to use BioRAM to conduct a comprehensive biosafety risk assessment for a laboratory performing monkey pox (mpox) diagnostics. The example provides practical guidance on identifying hazards, evaluating risks, and implementing appropriate mitigation measures.

<Note>
The complete assessment files are available in the BioRAM repository:
- **Excel Assessment**: `BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk) - MonkeyPox.xlsx`
- **Documentation**: `Monkey Pox Example.docx`
- **Updated Version**: `BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk) - MonkeyPox-updaated Dec 2023.xlsx`
</Note>

## Scenario Description

The example assessment focuses on a diagnostic laboratory that:

- Conducts monkey pox virus testing and diagnostics
- Processes clinical specimens from suspected cases
- Performs PCR-based molecular detection
- Handles infectious materials at BSL-2 or BSL-3 containment levels
- Requires appropriate biosafety controls and procedures

## Assessment Approach

The BioRAM 2022 (BioRisk) assessment methodology combines:

### 1. Hazard Identification

Systematic review of:
- Biological agent properties
- Laboratory processes and procedures
- Potential exposure routes
- Vulnerable populations

### 2. Risk Analysis

Quantitative evaluation using BioRAM scoring matrices:
- **Likelihood** of exposure or release
- **Consequence** severity if exposure occurs
- **Risk level** calculation (likelihood × consequence)

### 3. Control Measures Evaluation

Assessment of existing and additional controls:
- Primary containment (BSCs, safety equipment)
- Secondary containment (facility design)
- Administrative controls (SOPs, training)
- Personal protective equipment

<Info>
The BioRAM 2022 version uses an integrated biosafety and biosecurity approach, evaluating both safety risks to personnel and security risks related to intentional misuse.
</Info>

## Results and Risk Scores

The monkey pox example demonstrates:

- **Baseline Risk Assessment**: Initial risk levels before enhanced controls
- **Residual Risk Calculation**: Risk levels after implementing mitigation measures
- **Risk Acceptance Criteria**: Determining if residual risks are acceptable
- **Documentation**: Complete audit trail of risk decisions

Typical risk categories evaluated:

| Risk Category | Example Factors | Control Strategy |
|--------------|----------------|------------------|
| Exposure during specimen processing | Aerosol generation, splash hazard | BSC use, facial protection |
| Laboratory-acquired infection | Direct contact, needlestick | Training, engineering controls |
| Environmental contamination | Surface contamination, spills | Disinfection protocols |
| Inadequate containment | Equipment failure, procedural gaps | Preventive maintenance, audits |

## Mitigation Measures Recommended

The assessment identifies specific risk reduction strategies:

### Engineering Controls
- Use of Class II or III biological safety cabinets
- HEPA-filtered exhaust systems
- Sealed centrifuge rotors and safety cups
- Hands-free waste disposal systems

### Administrative Controls
- Standard operating procedures for high-risk tasks
- Personnel training and competency verification
- Medical surveillance programs
- Incident reporting and investigation procedures

### Personal Protective Equipment
- Laboratory coats or gowns
- Gloves (double gloving for high-risk procedures)
- Eye and face protection
- Respiratory protection when indicated

### Operational Practices
- Minimizing aerosol-generating procedures
- Working within biological safety cabinets
- Proper sharps handling and disposal
- Regular decontamination of work surfaces

## Using This Template

To adapt the monkey pox example for similar assessments:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the Example Files">
    Access `BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk) - MonkeyPox.xlsx` from the repository to review the complete assessment structure.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customize Agent Information">
    Replace monkey pox-specific data with information about your biological agent:
    - Pathogen characteristics
    - Risk group classification
    - Transmission routes
    - Recommended biosafety level
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Adapt Laboratory Context">
    Modify the assessment to reflect your facility:
    - Types of procedures performed
    - Available containment equipment
    - Staff training and experience
    - Facility infrastructure
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Evaluate and Score Risks">
    Use the BioRAM matrices to:
    - Assess likelihood and consequences
    - Calculate risk scores
    - Identify unacceptable risks
    - Prioritize mitigation efforts
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Document Decisions">
    Record your risk assessment:
    - Control measures selected
    - Residual risk levels
    - Acceptance decisions
    - Review and update schedule
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
This example is for training and reference purposes. Always conduct facility-specific risk assessments based on your actual operations, agent characteristics, and regulatory requirements.
