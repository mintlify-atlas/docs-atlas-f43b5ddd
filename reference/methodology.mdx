## Overview

BioRAM (Biosafety and Biosecurity Laboratory Risk Assessment Tool) provides a structured methodology for evaluating biological risks in laboratory settings. The tool uses a question-based approach to systematically assess safety and security risks.

## Scientific Basis

BioRAM's methodology is grounded in established biosafety and biosecurity principles, drawing from:

- **CDC Guidelines**: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention biosafety standards
- **WHO Laboratory Biosafety Manual**: World Health Organization best practices
- **International Biosafety Standards**: Globally recognized laboratory safety protocols
- **BMBL (Biosafety in Microbiological and Biomedical Laboratories)**: U.S. standard reference

The assessment framework was developed through collaboration between laboratory safety experts, biosecurity professionals, and risk assessment specialists.

<Info>
Detailed methodology and scientific validation are documented in the **BioRAM UserGuide SAND** report, available in the source distribution.
</Info>

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Dual Assessment Approach

BioRAM provides separate assessments for two distinct risk domains:

**Biosafety Assessment**
- Evaluates risks to laboratory personnel and the environment
- Focuses on accidental exposure and containment failures
- Considers pathogen characteristics, laboratory procedures, and safety controls
- Maps to Biosafety Level (BSL) requirements

**Biosecurity Assessment**
- Evaluates risks of intentional misuse or theft of biological materials
- Focuses on access control, inventory management, and personnel reliability
- Considers threat actors, material attractiveness, and security measures
- Identifies vulnerabilities in physical and operational security

### Integrated BioRisk Assessment (2022)

The BioRAM-2022 (BioRisk) version combines both safety and security assessments into a single integrated tool, recognizing that:

- Safety and security risks often overlap
- Comprehensive risk management requires addressing both domains
- Integrated assessment improves efficiency and identifies cross-cutting risks

## Question-Based Methodology

### Risk Factor Mapping

BioRAM uses structured questionnaires that map to specific risk factors:

1. **Agent Characteristics**
   - Pathogenicity and virulence
   - Route of transmission
   - Availability of prophylaxis or treatment
   - Environmental stability

2. **Laboratory Activities**
   - Procedures performed (culture, concentration, aerosolization)
   - Volumes and concentrations handled
   - Equipment and containment used
   - Frequency and duration of work

3. **Personnel Factors**
   - Training and competency
   - Immunization status
   - Medical surveillance programs
   - Safety culture and compliance

4. **Facility and Engineering Controls**
   - Containment barriers (primary and secondary)
   - Ventilation and air handling systems
   - Decontamination capabilities
   - Emergency response systems

5. **Administrative and Operational Controls**
   - Standard operating procedures
   - Access control and authorization
   - Inventory and accountability systems
   - Incident reporting and investigation

### Assessment Process

The methodology follows a systematic evaluation process:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define Scope">
    Identify the specific laboratory, agents, and procedures to be assessed
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete Questionnaire">
    Answer structured questions about risk factors and control measures
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Risk Evaluation">
    Tool processes responses to evaluate risk levels across different categories
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Results Analysis">
    Review risk scores, identify gaps, and prioritize mitigation actions
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Mitigation Planning">
    Develop action plans to address identified risks and deficiencies
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Risk Scoring and Evaluation

### Qualitative Risk Assessment

BioRAM employs a qualitative risk assessment approach that:

- Categorizes risks into levels (e.g., Low, Medium, High)
- Uses expert judgment encoded in the assessment logic
- Considers multiple factors simultaneously
- Provides context-specific risk characterization

### Risk Level Determination

Risk levels are determined through:

1. **Factor Weighting**: Different risk factors carry different weights based on their relative importance
2. **Combinatorial Analysis**: Multiple factors are evaluated together to determine overall risk
3. **Control Effectiveness**: Existing control measures reduce risk levels
4. **Residual Risk**: Final assessment reflects risk remaining after controls are applied

<Note>
BioRAM uses a decision-tree approach where answers to specific questions guide the assessment through different risk pathways, ensuring that the evaluation is tailored to the specific laboratory context.
</Note>

## Assessment Types and Applications

### When to Use Each Assessment

**Biosafety Assessment** - Use when:
- Conducting initial biosafety level determination
- Evaluating new procedures or agents
- Investigating incidents or near-misses
- Periodic review of laboratory operations
- Compliance verification

**Biosecurity Assessment** - Use when:
- Assessing Select Agent facilities
- Evaluating security vulnerabilities
- Implementing access control systems
- Personnel reliability program review
- Regulatory compliance (e.g., Federal Select Agent Program)

**Integrated BioRisk Assessment** - Use when:
- Comprehensive risk management is required
- Addressing both safety and security concerns
- Strategic planning and resource allocation
- Institutional risk assessment programs

## Validation and Quality Assurance

### Peer Review

The BioRAM methodology has undergone:

- Expert panel review by biosafety professionals
- Field testing in diverse laboratory settings
- International validation across different regulatory frameworks
- Continuous improvement based on user feedback

### Limitations and Considerations

Users should be aware that:

- Assessment results depend on accurate and complete information
- Professional judgment is required to interpret results
- BioRAM supports but does not replace expert risk assessment
- Local regulations and institutional policies may require additional considerations
- Results should be validated by qualified biosafety professionals

<Warning>
BioRAM is a decision-support tool. Final risk determinations and mitigation decisions should be made by qualified biosafety and biosecurity professionals with appropriate expertise.
</Warning>

## Documentation and Reporting

BioRAM supports comprehensive documentation:

- **Assessment Records**: Complete record of questions and answers
- **Risk Profiles**: Visual and textual summary of risk levels
- **Gap Analysis**: Identification of deficiencies and areas for improvement
- **Action Plans**: Recommended mitigation measures
- **Historical Tracking**: Ability to compare assessments over time

### Regulatory Compliance

Assessment documentation can support:

- Biosafety Committee review
- Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC) submissions
- Regulatory inspections and audits
- Accreditation processes
- Continuous improvement programs
